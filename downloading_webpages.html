How To "Hack" Webpages

What is Hacking?

I feel that a lot of non-technical people or anyone born before 1995 think that hacking is the use of esoteric technical knowledge to steal information or make machines do things outside of their design. While this is a fiction of Hollywood, they are not entirely wrong.

This is the part of the class where I ask everyone what they think hacking is. There's usually a 5 second pause of silence, then 2 or 3 of you raise their hand because most of you are either tired or don't want to look too eager on the first day.

But thankfully, this is an online class, so I'll spare you the ordeal of collaborative learning by telling you what it means in this context.

Hacking is the use of technincal knowledge to achieve a desired outcome.

Your First Hacking Activity

Sometimes, I will ask you to download and edit webpages. We're going to start by reading the code in this web page.

Right-click on this webpage.

Select "view webpage" This should open a new tab in your browser. In the new tab, read through the code.

There should be a HIDDEN MESSAGE inside the code. Follow its directions. When you are done, go to the next lesson.
