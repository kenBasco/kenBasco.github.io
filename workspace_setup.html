Workspaces

An Analogy on getting dressed

I have trouble getting dressed sometimes.

The pants I want to wear for the day is somewhere in my room and other times it's hanging in the bathroom.

I can't even remember which pants I left my wallet and keys in, so I end up hunting every article of clothing in my house to find it.

Then, I have to put my pants, wallet, keys, shirt, etc all together.

I feel like it would have been really nice if I had everything in one place.

Programming is like getting dressed

When you write your code in all these different files, they sometimes need to talk to eachother. As a programmer, it becomes difficult keeping track of what file (clothing) is in what folder (room), let alone getting dressed when all your clothes are in different places.

Setting Up Your Workspace

Create a new folder on your computer. This is going to be your workspace. It can be anywhere, as long as it is easy to remember. Personally, I'm going to put my folder on my Desktop.

Right Click

Select "New..."

Click "Folder"

A text box should appear under the folder.

Start typing what you want to name your workspace and hit "Enter" on your keyboard. I'm going to name it "Smattering Workspace" so I remember it's for this class.

Throughout this class, you are going to be downloading, editing, and testing files.

Some of these files compliment one another, in that they need to talk to eachother.

So, your workspace is a nice little room you can put them in to have a chat !
